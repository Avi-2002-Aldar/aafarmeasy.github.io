Kisan Credit Card (KCC)


Apply to scheme: Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
Purpose: The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) is a government scheme in India that aims to provide timely and affordable credit to farmers to meet their agricultural needs, including cultivation expenses, post-harvest costs, and working capital requirements.
Eligibility: All farmers, including small and marginal farmers, tenant farmers, sharecroppers, and self-help groups, are eligible to apply for a Kisan Credit Card.
Financial Support: KCC provides access to credit facilities based on the farmer's credit requirements, which are determined by the scale of farming, cropping pattern, and other factors. It provides a revolving credit line that can be used multiple times within the validity period.
Features: KCC acts as a single-window credit facility, incorporating crop loans, consumption loans, and term loans for agriculture-related purposes. It helps simplify credit access and reduce the need for multiple loan applications.
Validity: The KCC has a validity period, usually of up to five years, which can be renewed upon review and assessment.
Credit Limit: The credit limit under the KCC is determined based on the farmer's cropping pattern and the scale of operations. It helps ensure that farmers have access to sufficient credit for their agricultural activities.
Interest Rates: The interest rates for KCC loans are typically lower compared to other forms of credit, making it more affordable for farmers.
Flexibility: Farmers can choose the timing and amount of withdrawal within the credit limit, providing flexibility based on their actual needs.
Repayment: KCC loans are generally structured with flexible repayment options, allowing farmers to repay the loan after the sale of their crops.
Implementation: KCC is implemented by various banks and financial institutions in coordination with the National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD).
Subsidy: In some cases, farmers may also be eligible for interest rate subsidies and other incentives, depending on the government's policy and the farmer's credit history.
Insurance Coverage: Many KCCs come with insurance coverage, providing financial protection to farmers in case of crop failure, accidents, or other unforeseen events.
