National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA)


Apply to scheme: National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA)
Objective: The National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture aims to promote sustainable agricultural practices that enhance productivity, ensure resource efficiency, and strengthen the resilience of Indian agriculture to climate change.
Focus Areas: NMSA focuses on various components including soil health management, water-use efficiency, crop diversification, promotion of organic farming, and climate-resilient agricultural practices.
Soil Health Management: The mission emphasizes improving soil health through balanced nutrient management, adopting organic practices, and encouraging the use of organic and green manures.
Water Use Efficiency: NMSA aims to improve water-use efficiency by promoting efficient irrigation practices, micro-irrigation techniques, and water-saving technologies.
Crop Diversification: The mission encourages farmers to diversify their crop choices to reduce risks and enhance income stability. Crop diversification also contributes to better soil health and resource utilization.
Organic Farming: NMSA supports the adoption of organic farming practices, including the use of bio-fertilizers, bio-pesticides, and other eco-friendly inputs to reduce chemical dependency.
Climate Resilient Agriculture: The mission addresses the challenges posed by climate change by promoting climate-resilient agricultural practices.This includes the use of drought-resistant varieties, conservation agriculture, and agroforestry.
Implementation: NMSA is implemented through various central and state agencies, with coordination at both levels to ensure effective execution of sustainable agriculture practices.
Training and Capacity Building: The mission focuses on training and capacity building of farmers to adopt new practices and technologies related to sustainable agriculture.
Funding: NMSA receives funding from the Government of India and is a part of the National Action Plan on Climate Change.
